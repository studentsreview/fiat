It's not eye-opening or awe-inspiring but it is enjoyable. Ms. Freeland has a perpetually exhausted demeanor that I relate to. 80% of this class is reading as a group and then voluntary discussion. She does the occasional reading quiz but those are fairly easy. She will have one essay and one in-class essay. You will get a good enough grade on the essay as long as you follow her directions about structure, I wouldn't get my hopes up about an A though. I enjoyed the class because of the in-class discussions. There will be days when everyone just vibes and talks about the book with no real direction to the conversation. Those days are great. The one reason some people might be hesitant to take this class is that you will have to perform at least two skits in a semester, she is very fond of them. As long as you are doing the reading and the work in class you will be fine and probably end the class with an A or a high B.
